Eye bolts for hanging

What you'll need

  • Tools

    • Power Drill

  • Materials

    • 8 - Eye bolts

    • 8 - Nuts

    • 8 - Washer

Instructions

Drill 8 holes, 4 on each of the 2 top PVC pipes. They should be 1/4" wide with about 12" between each hole. Drill from top to bottom. All the way through the pipe. Thread the eye bolts through the pipes with the eye hole on the bottom. Cap the top with a washer under a nut. It should look like this:

Drilling bucket holes

What you'll need

  • Tools

    • Power Drill

  • Materials

    • 3 - Buckets (5 gallon)

    • 1 - Bucket lid (5 gallon)

Note

Of the 3 buckets, 1 will be your nutrient solution reservoir, 2 will be for plants. When specifying drill hole locations on the bucket sides, we'll use an angle coordinate (see diagram below) and a distance from top or bottom.


Drilling holes in reservoir bucket and lid

  • In the center of the plain bucket lid, drill a 1/2" hole

  • Drill 1/2" holes in bucket side at 0° and 180°. Make it so the bottom of the hole is just about touching the bottom of the inside of the bucket.

Drilling holes in plant buckets

  • Drill 1" hole centered in the bottom of the bucket

  • Drill 1/2" hole in bucket side at 0°, 3.5" from top lip of bucket.

  • Drill 1/8" holes in bucket side at 45°, 135°, 225° and 315°, .375" from top lip of bucket

  • Drill pairs of 1/8" holes spiraling around bucket side for zip ties, one on top of the other with 1/4" between.

    • At 45°, with pair center 3.5" from top lip of bucket

    • At 90°, with pair center 3.25" from top lip of bucket

    • At 135°, with pair center 3" from top lip of bucket

    • At 180°, with pair center 2.625" from top lip of bucket

    • At 225°, with pair center 2.375" from top lip of bucket

    • At 270°, with pair center 2.125" from top lip of bucket

    • At 315°, with pair center 1.875" from top lip of bucket

    • At 0°, with pair center 1.5" from top lip of bucket

    • At 45°, with pair center 1.25" from top lip of bucket

  • Repeat on other plant bucket

Attaching pump tubing

What you'll need

  • Tools

    • Power Drill

    • Glue Gun

    • Box Cutter

  • Materials

    • 2 - Funnels (32mm/6.4mm)

    • 1 tube - Caulk (Water proof)

    • 12 feet - Tubing (7/16" outside, 5/16" inside)

    • 2 - tubing end cap

    • 8 - Spray nozzles (180 Degree)

    • 18 - Zip ties

Instructions

Take the tubing (7/16" outside, 5/16" inside) and put the end cap on it. Bring the tube into the bucket with the end cap pointed counterclockwise. Attach the tube to the inside of the bucket by threading the zip tie into the top-most pair of 1/8" holes, around the tube, then back out again. Repeat this with the other 1/8" hole pairs to connect more of the tube until you run out of hole pairs. Then thread the tube out of the bucket, through the 1/2" hole at 0°. Cut the tube so that there is 7" of slack outside the bucket.

Zip ties from inside:

Drill 4 small 1/8" holes through the top layer, not all the way through, of the tubing inside the bucket at 0°, 90°, 180° and 270°. Put spray nozzles into each of these holes with the sprayers pointing towards the center of the bucket.

Repeat this for the other plant bucket.

Cut a 22" piece of tubing and thread it through the reservoir bucket lid. Leave 7" of slack outside the lid.

Hanging the buckets

What you'll need

  • Tools

    • Box Cutter

  • Materials

    • Twine

Instructions

Cut 4 pieces of twine, 9' each. On each plant bucket, thread one strand into the 1/8" hole at 45° and out the hole at 315°. Thread the other strand through the holes at 135° and 225°. You should have 4' slack coming out both ends of the bucket.

Place the plant buckets next to one another inside the framing, centered, with about 1' between them. Rotate the buckets so the slack tubing points towards the other bucket. Put the slack twine through the eye bolts and pull to hoist up the buckets. Get the buckets 8" above the ground, then tie the twine to the eye bolts to keep it at that height.

Use the top of the eye bolts to create two X's with the twine. This will be used to hang the grow lights.




Connecting plant buckets to reservoir

What you'll need

  • Tools

    • Glue Gun

    • Box Cutter

  • Materials

    • 1 tube - Caulk (Water proof)

    • 12 feet - Tubing (7/16" outside, 5/16" inside)

    • 1 - tubing T connector

    • 1 - Bucket basket lid (10" wide)

    • 1 - Bucket basket lid (6" wide)

Instructions

Use glue gun to attach top of funnels to bottom of plant buckets.

Once dried, use glue gun to attach 1' pieces of tubing to bottom of funnels.



Put the reservoir bucket on the ground between the two hanging plant buckets. Rotate the reservoir so the holes on the sides are under the adjacent plant bucket. On both sides, take the tubing attached to the funnel and push about 1/2" of it into the hole in the side of the reservoir. Use glue gun, then waterproof caulk to seal hole.

Connect the slack tubing from the top of the reservoir and the sides of the plant buckets using the T connector.

Place basket lids (6" and 10") on plant buckets. Close up all unsealed holes with glue gun.
